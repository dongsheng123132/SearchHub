SearchHub is a multi‑engine search tool that opens your queries across multiple search engines in organized tab groups. It streamlines research by combining AI search, traditional search, developer resources, social and video platforms.

## Features
- **One input → multiple results**: Simultaneously search across Google, ChatGPT, Claude, and more.
- **Organized Chrome Tab Groups**: Automatically groups search results by query to keep your workspace clean.
- **Vertical Sidebar**: View and manage your search tabs efficiently in a side panel.
- **Quick Combos**: One-click access to popular engine sets (e.g., "AI Chat", "Dev Tools").
- **Custom Engines**: Add your own search engines and save your preferences.
- **Keyboard Shortcuts**: 
  - `Alt+S`: Open SearchHub
  - `Alt+Q`: Search selected text immediately

## How It Works
1. Click the extension icon or press `Alt+S`.
2. Select your desired engines (or use a Quick Combo).
3. Enter your query and hit Search.
4. Results open in a new Tab Group, keeping your browser organized.
5. Use the sidebar to manage open tabs and groups.

## Permissions
SearchHub respects your privacy and requests only the minimum permissions needed to function:

- `tabs` & `tabGroups`: To create new tabs for your search results and organize them into groups.
- `sidePanel`: To display the vertical tabs sidebar for better management.
- `scripting`: To read the text you select when you use the "Search selected text" feature (Alt+Q).
- `notifications`: To show small alerts (e.g., if you try to search without selecting an engine).
- `system.display`: To optimize the window layout when opening multiple results.

**Note on Site Access**: 
SearchHub does **NOT** read or change data on all websites. It only interacts with specific search engine domains (like google.com, chatgpt.com) to perform the searches you initiate.

## Privacy Policy
- **No Tracking**: We do not collect, store, or transmit your personal data.
- **Local Storage**: Your settings (custom engines, theme preferences) are stored locally on your device using `chrome.storage`.
- **Transparency**: The extension only runs when you explicitly trigger a search.
